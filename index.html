MEMBER ASSOCIATE TRAINING GUIDE

5-Day Comprehensive Training Program

PROGRAM OVERVIEW

This five-day training program will prepare you to excel as a Member Associate at Scenthound. You'll learn everything from customer interactions and booking systems to membership sales and handling challenging situations. By the end of Day 5, you'll be certified and ready to work independently.

DAY 1

Drop Off and Pick Up / FOSA Basics

Training Objectives Today:

  • Dog handoffs
  • Kennel Management
  • Scenter Experience Map
  • Menu boards
  • Lobby etiquette
  • Knowledge wall
  • MyTime schedule
  • Drop off/Pick up process
  • Member engagement
  • Membership sales
  • House rules

Part 1: Drop Off

Tools to Review with Trainee:

  • Employee Experience Map
  • Brand DNA
  • Scenter Experience Map
  • Laminates
  • Daily Checklists
  • MyTime Symbol Quick Guide
  • Knowledge Wall
  • FOSA SOP Binder

Drop Off Process for Members:

  1. Stand and greet customer by name when they walk in, then greet pet
  2. Quick assessment
  3. Confirm services and time
  4. Confirm contact info
  5. Transfer and take dog back
  6. Weight
  7. Into kennel

Training Steps for Drop Off:

  • Start by going over Member Experience Map and FOS tools
  • Give a quick breakdown of what the drop off process for a member is supposed to look like
  • Manager and MA do a quick role play and allow for questions and interjections - take your time and explain each step as you are doing it
  • Shadow a couple of drop offs
  • Have a quick debrief about what you saw, what you thought worked, what maybe we could improve

Part 2: Pick Up

Tools to Review with Trainee:

  • SCENT Check
  • Scenter Experience Map
  • Laminates
  • Knowledge Wall
  • Educational Trifolds

Pick Up Process for Members:

  1. Stand to greet pet parent
  2. Go over SCENT check
  3. Make relevant recommendations
  4. Check out and rebook
  5. Bring dog out

Training Steps for Pick Up:

  • Go over house rules
  • Give an intro to MyTime booking - show them how to book a new appointment and do basic navigation
  • Give a quick breakdown of what the pick-up process for a member is supposed to look like
  • Chat about mid-appointment texts and being able to keep an eye on time while also tracking FOSA tasks
  • Have Manager and MA roleplay a pick up and allow for questions and interjections - take your time and explain each step
  • Here we should be going over how to talk about SCENT score, how to recommend products and add-on services, and how to rebook appointments
  • Shadow a couple of pick ups
  • Have a quick debrief about what you saw, what you thought worked, what maybe we could improve

END OF DAY RECAP

Part 3: Drop Off/Pick Up Review, FOSA Training

Tools to Review with Trainee:

  • FOSA SOP Document
  • FOSA App
  • Telephone Inquiry Process
  • Breed Book

Training Steps to Review Drop Off/Pick Up:

  • Review drop off basics, then roleplay drop off basics
  • Review house rules
  • Review booking in MyTime
  • Review pick up basics, then roleplay pick up basics

Training Steps for FOSA:

  • Start by explaining what resources we use up front (FOSA, MyTime, Zoho, email, and scripts) and their role in ensuring a smooth FOS experience
  • Take some time to make sure everyone can log in and out of all systems and is comfortable doing so
  • Go over Telephone Inquiry process
  • Go over FOSA Procedure guide - explain each step briefly and show how to do in the system
  • Start with new leads, and have Manager and MA roleplay a call for a new lead wanting to book an appointment - take the time to explain each step and allow for questions and interjections
  • Have each member practice booking an appointment on a future date with Reilly or Farrah's account to physically go through the process (make sure we are deleting these appointments as well so we aren't filling up the schedule!)
  • Do some voicemail roleplaying as well - engaging conversations are our bread and butter but being able to leave solid voicemails without sounding like a robot is an important skill to get comfortable with
  • Have each member do a New Lead call
  • Follow up by going through the same process with Active Trial Leads
  • Manager and MA roleplay a call for an active trial lead wanting to book an appointment - take the time to explain each step and allow for questions and interjections
  • Follow up by pairing managers with newbies for some roleplaying to allow for practice and feedback
  • Have each member practice booking an appointment on a future date (yes again, repetition is how we build the muscle memory!) with Reilly or Farrah's account
  • Have each member do an Active Trial Lead call
  • For the other steps, simply go through the process with them and have them show you which steps and places they need to go to complete the task
  • Debrief - wins, losses, reflections, and highlights!

Part 4: Drop Off/Pick Up Review

Training Steps for Drop Off/Pick Up Review:

  • Manager shadow newbies through drop offs and pick ups in the morning
  • Debrief after each drop off/pick up to reflect and give feedback
  • Reiterate importance of keeping track of the schedule so we can do mid-appointment texts and ensure we are having prompt pick up for pups
  • If extra downtime, keep roleplaying and work through more nuanced scenarios

Example Scenarios to Practice:

  • "My dog is rabies exempt"
  • "I need a haircut TODAY"
  • "I have a severely matted doodle and I just want to come in for a bath. I can only come in at 5:30 pm, when is your next available?"
Get them comfortable with not knowing. The goal here is not to have them learn everything, but to help them learn confidence. You may not have the answer but you know someone who does, or maybe you need to take a second to look that up. Things are always changing, and Seek Growth is one of our pillars - if we ever feel like we know everything, we're failing on that count. Being open and transparent is part of what makes Scenthound unique!

Part 5: FOSA Review

Training Steps:

  • Manager shadow a couple of tasks from each segment of FOSA
  • Debrief after each call to reflect and give feedback

Part 6: Trial Drop Off

Tools to Review with Trainee:

  • Trial Guide
  • Trial Drop Off Guide
  • Knowledge Wall
  • Laminates

Trial Drop Off Process:

  1. Stand and greet customer by name when they walk in, then greet pet
  2. More thorough assessment
  3. Start with waiver
  4. Explain services and emphasize towel dry, give Future Member brochure
  5. Confirm shampoo
  6. Confirm time and contact info
  7. Ask if they have any questions
  8. Transfer and take dog back
  9. Weight
  10. Into kennel

Training Steps for Trial Drop Offs:

  • Take some time to explain how they differ, and really emphasize how important these are
  • This is how we grow, this is how we get new members!
  • This is also where we are setting expectations for future visits
  • We need to know and be comfortable enforcing lobby rules
  • We also need to be familiar and confident with our assessments to help set a positive tone for both the pet and parent
  • Manager and MA roleplay and allow for questions and interjections - take your time and explain each step
  • Roleplay trial drop off with trainee
  • Shadow trial drop off if you can

Part 7: Trial Pick Up

Tools to Review with Trainee:

  • Trial Pick Up Guide
  • Houndswell Product Sheets

Trial Pick Up Process:

  1. Greet
  2. Pitch
  3. SCENT Check - explanation and breakdown (start with 5's and work down)
  4. Transition - "We don't want to see trends like these continue, that's why we have our monthly memberships"
  5. Recommendation of membership - try the Goldilocks method, HIGHLIGHT KEY 4 IN PLUS
  6. Closing pitch - "We loved Sparky so much, we're gonna waive that registration fee for you today"
  7. Let. It. Simmer. (Be quiet, no need to say more, they will let you know if they have questions)
  8. Once they have signed up (or declined), check out and rebook
  9. Bring dog out
THIS IS THE BIGGEST DEAL! This is how we grow, this is how we get new members. We want to make sure we are hitting every step of the pick up process.

Training Steps for Trial Pick Up:

  • Take some time to explain how they differ (yes again, this is THE BIGGEST DEAL!!!) and really emphasize how important these are
  • This is how we grow, this is how we get new members
  • We want to make sure we are hitting every step of the pick up process
  • Take a bit to explain the pitch and give examples, then have them try
  • Go over why each step is important and set a goal that by end of day Friday everyone will have their pitch (Use Trial Pick Up Guide)
  • Have manager and MA roleplay and allow for questions and interjections - take your time and explain each step
  • Roleplay Trial pick up with trainee
  • Shadow trial pick up if you can

END OF DAY RECAP

Review daily learning and temp check

DAY 4

Training Wheels

Training Objectives Today:

  • Independence with basics of drop off/pick up
  • Independence with basics of FOSA/Phones
  • Independence with booking
  • Overcoming objections
  • Conflict resolution

Step 8: Challenging Situations

Tools to Review with Trainee:

  • Workdrive
  • Membership Cancellation SOP

Training Steps for Challenging Situations:

  • Cancellations should be handled by managers exclusively - go over process and explain what they should do if someone requests a cancellation while a manager is not present
  • Should let them know manager will follow up with them ASAP
  • Discuss the concept of pain flushing and the benefit of silence
  • Get to the root of the "why" of the objection, address it, and put it on them
  • Roleplay to practice pain flushing and discuss opportunities and feedback after each one
  • Roleplay objections and discuss opportunities and feedback after each one

Step 9: Trainee Shadow Shift

Training Steps for Trainee Shadow Day:

  • Go over the certification for independent MA and explain what each item means
  • Allow room for questions so they can understand what their expectations are
  • For the rest of the shift, the manager should shadow the MA as they work relatively independently and work on fine-tuning
  • Find learning opportunities on the fly and work on going through certification
  • Coach on things that need improvement
  • Praise the things they have down

END OF DAY RECAP

  • Review daily learning and temp check
  • Check in with them at the end of the day with their certification
  • Go over the expectations for independent again
  • Let them know what they passed on, reiterate what you need to see to get them signed off tomorrow

DAY 5

Training Wheels Off

Training Objectives Today:

  • MA Independence Evaluation
  • MA Certification

Step 10: Certification

Certification Process:

  • Review the certification again
  • Manager shadow MA all day, as hands off as possible
  • Give support if needed but encourage them to utilize their tools to answer their questions
  • Roleplay hard in downtime:
    • Ring ring
    • Save me
    • Sell me
    • Teach me

END OF DAY RECAP

  • Review daily learning and temp check
  • Sign off on certification OR make a plan for next week to work on what needs to be worked on
